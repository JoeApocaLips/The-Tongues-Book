  {\huge\bfseries Notice} \\
  \vspace{1.8em}

  \small
  This book is not \textit{The Tongue Machine}. \\
  \vspace{0.3em}
  \textit{The Tongue Machine} (published by Schism Press) is a serious work. It concerns language, pedagogy,
  power, the slow catastrophe of education. Ansgar Allen wrote it.
  You should read it. \\
  \vspace{0.3em}
  This book concerns tongues. \textbf{Only tongues.} \\
  \vspace{0.3em}
  Not the organ of language --- the organ itself. Wet. Extended.
  Occasionally pierced. Photographed by strangers in bathrooms,
  under fluorescent light, in front of mirrors, against neutral walls. \\
  \vspace{0.3em}
  Allen made a video. A gallery of tongues, filmed close. The tongue
  as object. The tongue detached from speech, from meaning, from the
  machine that runs on it. \\
  \vspace{0.3em}
  In February 2026, Ansgar Allen issued a call for tongues. Email a
  photo. Full face or just the mouth. B\&W. Each to appear for a fraction
  of a second. \\
  \vspace{0.3em}
  We did not email... \\
  We took that literally. \\
  \vspace{0.3em}
  Each image in this book was generated by a machine. Parameters were
  set --- pose, expression, framing, lighting, background, piercing,
  artifact --- and sampled at random. The machine decided. Two hundred
  times. No photographer. No subject. No session. A pipeline running
  in the dark, producing tongues on demand. \\
  \vspace{0.3em}
  The tongue machine, then. But inverted: not the organ that produces
  language, but a language --- a prompt --- that produces organs. \\
  \vspace{0.8em}
  Each image carries the residue of its own production. The AI model
  brought its own biases --- aesthetic preferences, demographic defaults,
  cultural assumptions baked into training data. It hallucinated where
  it didn't know. It standardized where it should have varied.
  The pipeline decided; the author selected. What remains is neither
  documentary nor fiction --- it is the negotiated output of a machine
  that never saw a tongue, and a process that never had a subject. \\
  \vspace{0.8em}
  Two hundred tongues. No meaning guaranteed. \\
  \vspace{1.2em}
  \textit{The Mass Factory, March 2026.}
